Stay Safe, Stay Informed - Your Complete Guide to COVID-19

Important Notice

Stay updated with the latest COVID-19 guidelines from health authorities. Follow safety protocols and get vaccinated to protect yourself and others.

What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It primarily spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. The virus can cause mild to severe respiratory illness.

While most people infected with COVID-19 experience mild to moderate symptoms, some may develop serious illness requiring medical attention. Older adults and people with underlying health conditions are at higher risk.

Common Symptoms

COVID-19 symptoms can appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus. Common symptoms include:

Fever

Cough

Shortness of Breath

Fatigue

Headache

Body Aches

Loss of Taste/Smell

Sore Throat

Prevention Guidelines

Protect yourself and others by following these essential prevention measures:

Wash Your Hands

Wash hands frequently with soap for at least 20 seconds

Wear a Mask

Wear a face mask in crowded or enclosed spaces

Social Distance

Maintain 6 feet distance from others in public

Sanitize

Use hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol

Stay Home

Stay home if you feel sick or have symptoms

Get Vaccinated

Get vaccinated to protect yourself and others

Treatment & Management

If you test positive for COVID-19:

  • Self-isolate for at least 5-10 days depending on symptoms
  • Rest and stay hydrated
  • Monitor your symptoms daily
  • Take medications as prescribed by your doctor
  • Seek immediate medical attention if symptoms worsen

Seek emergency care if you experience:

  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Persistent chest pain or pressure
  • Confusion or inability to stay awake
  • Bluish lips or face
  • Severe or persistent vomiting
